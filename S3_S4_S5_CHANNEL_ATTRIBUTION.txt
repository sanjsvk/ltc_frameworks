RECOMMENDATION FOR PAPER

Section: "Channel-Level Attribution as Model Validation"

Aggregate LTC recovery is necessary but insufficient for model evaluation. Practitioners should validate
that recovered channel rankings match domain knowledge (TV & Video dominance). This analysis reveals:

1. **F2 models (ARDL, Koyck)** systematically invert channel rankings across S2-S5, indicating
   fundamental confusion about per-channel LTC structure despite reasonable aggregate recovery.

2. **F1 models (Static Adstock)** fail catastrophically on seasonal confounding and permanent shifts,
   with channel attributions degrading faster than aggregate metrics suggest.

3. **F3 Fixed-Decay models (Kalman_dlm, BSTS)** lose video LTC signal under any scenario variation
   (seasonality, structural breaks, weak signal), indicating the fixed decay assumption is incompatible
   with production MMM uncertainty.

4. **MCMC (mcmc_stock)** is the only model preserving correct channel ranking under scenario variation,
   with TV & Video dominance maintained even under weak signal (S5 with manual init).

**Practical Implication:** Channel-level validation should be mandatory before deploying any MMM model.
Aggregate metrics can mask 50-90% of channel-level failure, leading to severely biased budget allocation.
MCMC's channel-level correctness under variation argues for its adoption in production systems.
